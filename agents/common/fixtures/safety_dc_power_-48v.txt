Site Safety Procedure — -48V DC Power Plant Servicing

Section 2 — Lockout / Tagout and PPE before servicing

Before any work on the -48V DC power plant, isolate the plant and apply
lockout/tagout. The -48V DC bus is nominally low voltage but delivers very
high fault current: an uncleared short across the battery bus can vaporize
tools and cause arc-flash burns.

Mandatory steps:
  1. Notify the NOC and open a maintenance window for the site.
  2. Open and lock the battery disconnect and the load distribution breaker
     serving the equipment to be worked on; apply your personal tag.
  3. Verify zero energy: measure the isolated segment with a meter before
     touching any busbar; confirm it reads near 0 V.
  4. Wear the required PPE: insulated gloves rated for the bus, arc-rated
     face shield, and remove all metal jewelry and watches.
  5. Keep one insulated tool in use at a time; never bridge the positive
     return and the -48V bus with an uninsulated tool.
Only after verified isolation and PPE may the rectifier or battery work begin.
